BEE STORY
01
General Characteristics of Bees

The bee is an insect belonging to the order Hymenoptera, characterized by its small size and a body divided into three main parts: head, thorax, and abdomen. They have thin, transparent wings and sensitive antennae that help them sense their environment. Tiny hairs on their bodies assist bees in collecting pollen and communicating with one another.

02
THE ROLE

Bees play an extremely important role in ecosystems due to their ability to pollinate plants. As they collect nectar and pollen, bees inadvertently transfer pollen from one flower to another, enabling plants to grow and produce fruits. Without bees, many ecosystems would suffer significant disruptions, leading to decreased agricultural productivity.

03
Common Types of Bees

Honey bees (Apis mellifera): These bees produce honey and have a highly organized social structure, consisting of queens, workers, and drones. Bumblebees (Bombus spp.): Larger in size, they often work individually or in small groups. Wasps (Vespa spp.): Though not honey producers, they play a role in controlling pests
